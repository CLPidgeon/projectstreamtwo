What do that charts show?

This page aims to outline some of the trends the charts display, from who the big spenders are to the general spending habits of Premier League clubs. We also look at the types of transfers Premier League clubs use.

Spending Increase

From the first chart we can see that there has been a general steady increase in net spend from Premier League clubs, this has grown by nearly half a billion pounds in the past 10 seasons from just under 300 billion to over 700 million Euro. This indicates that Premier League clubs are spending more and more money on players outside of the league.

Big Spenders

If we look through the transfer histories of various teams we can see that Manchester City are far and away the leagues biggest spenders in the past 10 seasons with an eye-watering net spend of close to 1 BILLION Euro (845M). This is more than double their closest net spending rival, Manchester United, on 418M Euro.

Transfer Values

From the row chart if we toggle through the seasons we can see that there were only 4 transfers of the value 20M or above in the 2006/07 transfer windows, this has more than quadrupled to 18 in the 2015/16 transfer windows with 4 of them being 50M or above.

By toggling through the seasons we can see the overall trend of an increase in general of transfer values.

Transfer Types

The transfer type pie chart shows us that loans with fees are very rarely used by Premier League clubs, the proportion of loans with fees in relation to full transfers has held steady over the years. There was however an increase in loans with fees in the 2013/14 season which hit almost 1/5 of transfers.
